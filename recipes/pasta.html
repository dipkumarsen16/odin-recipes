Description

Pasta is a staple food of Italian origin made from wheat flour and water, sometimes enriched with eggs. It comes in countless shapes—like spaghetti, penne, or ravioli—each designed to pair with different sauces or cooking styles. Pasta can be served fresh or dried, boiled or baked, and is celebrated for its versatility in dishes ranging from simple olive oil tosses to rich, layered lasagnas.

Beyond being a meal, pasta carries cultural weight as a symbol of Italian cuisine and hospitality. With a history dating back to ancient times, it has become a global comfort food enjoyed in homes and restaurants alike. Its adaptability makes it a canvas for creativity, whether in traditional recipes or modern fusion dishes.

Ingredients

  • Pasta (penne or any type)
  • Butter
  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Milk
  • Grated cheese (mozzarella or processed)
  • Chilli flakes
  • Oregano
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic (optional)

Steps

  1. Boil pasta in salted water until soft, then drain and keep aside.
  2. Heat butter in a pan on low flame.
  3. Add flour (maida) and mix well to form a smooth paste (roux).
  4. Slowly pour milk while stirring to avoid lumps.
  5. Cook until the sauce becomes thick and creamy.
  6. Add grated cheese and mix until it melts completely.
  7. Add salt, black pepper, chilli flakes, and oregano to taste.
  8. Add the boiled pasta and coat it well with the white cheese sauce.
  9. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes and serve hot.
